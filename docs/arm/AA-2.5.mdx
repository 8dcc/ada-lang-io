<p>[A <code><a href="./AA-2.5#S0015">character_literal</a></code> is formed by enclosing a graphic character between two apostrophe characters.] </p>

#### Syntax

<p><code>character_literal</code><a id="S0015"></a><code> ::= </code>'<code>graphic_character</code>'</p>
<p>NOTE 1   A <code><a href="./AA-2.5#S0015">character_literal</a></code> is an enumeration literal of a character type. See 3.5.2. </p>

#### Examples

<p><em>Examples of character literals:</em> </p>
<CodeBlock>
{"{"}{"AI95-00433-01"}{"}"} 'A'     '*'     '''     ' '{"\n"}'L'     ''     ''    -- Various els.{"\n"}''     ''            -- Big numbers - infinity and aleph.{"\n"}
</CodeBlock>


#### Wording Changes from Ada 83

<p>{"{"}<em>{"AI05-0299-1"}</em>{"}"} The definitions of the values of literals are in Clauses 3 and 4, rather than here, since it requires knowledge of types. </p>
